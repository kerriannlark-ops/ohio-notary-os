# Ohio Notary Commission to Revenue SOP

## START HERE
1. File the Ohio commission application.
2. Wait for approval.
3. Complete the oath.
4. Obtain the seal.
5. Finish the first-appointment launch kit.
6. Start with low-risk in-person revenue.

---

## 1. Immediate filing step
File at [notary.ohiosos.gov](https://notary.ohiosos.gov/).

Prepare this exact filing set:
- signature sample
- BCI report
- provider proof of education/testing

Time gates to keep visible:
- BCI should still be within **6 months** at filing
- education/testing proof should still be within **12 months** at filing

---

## 2. After approval
Do not perform notarial acts until all three are true:
- commission approved
- oath completed
- seal obtained

Internal OS rule:
- keep a traditional journal even though Ohio does not require one for traditional acts

---

## 3. First appointment launch kit
Before the first paid appointment, have all of these ready:
- traditional journal
- loose acknowledgment and jurat certificates
- intake form
- fee sheet
- travel-zone policy
- invoice template
- review-request text
- mileage tracker

---

## 5. What counts as another license?
### Real Ohio legal authorizations
- Ohio notary commission
- Ohio remote online notarization (RON) authorization

### Not separate Ohio notary licenses
- electronic in-person notarization = tooling upgrade, not a separate commission
- notary signing agent = industry credential stack, not an Ohio SOS license
- apostille support = admin/process service, not a notary license
- I-9 authorized representative = non-notarial service; do not use a notary seal

---
